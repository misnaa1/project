Bachelor of Social Work (BSW)

Course Overview

The Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) is a 3-year undergraduate degree course that focuses on the study of social work and community development.

Eligibility Criteria

  • 10+2 with any stream (Arts, Commerce, Science)
  • Minimum 50% marks in aggregate
  • Some colleges may require an entrance exam or interview

Course Structure

The BSW course is divided into 6 semesters, with a range of subjects to choose from, including:

  • Introduction to Social Work
  • Human Behavior and Social Environment
  • Social Work Practice
  • Community Development
  • Research Methods in Social Work
  • Field Work and Practicum

Specializations

Some popular specializations in BSW include:

  • Child and Family Welfare
  • Community Development
  • Mental Health
  • Disability Studies
  • Women's Studies

Career Opportunities

BSW graduates can pursue careers in:

  • Social work and counseling
  • Community development and organization
  • Child and family welfare
  • Mental health and disability services
  • Non-profit and voluntary organizations

Top Institutes

Some top institutes for BSW include:

  • Tata Institute of Social Sciences (TISS), Mumbai
  • Delhi School of Social Work, University of Delhi
  • Christ University, Bangalore
  • Madras School of Social Work, Chennai

Salary Range

The salary range for BSW graduates varies based on experience and industry, but here are some approximate figures:

  • Entry-level: ₹ 1,50,000 - ₹ 3,00,000 per annum
  • Mid-level: ₹ 3,50,000 - ₹ 6,00,000 per annum
  • Senior-level: ₹ 8,00,000 - ₹ 12,00,000 per annum
