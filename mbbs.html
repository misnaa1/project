Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS)

Overview

The Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) is a 5.5-year undergraduate degree that focuses on the study of medicine and surgery.

Eligibility Criteria

  • 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as compulsory subjects
  • Minimum 50% marks in aggregate
  • Qualifying exam scores (NEET, AIIMS, JIPMER, etc.)

Course Structure

The MBBS course is divided into 4 parts:

  • Pre-clinical (1st and 2nd year)
  • Para-clinical (3rd year)
  • Clinical (4th and 5th year)
  • Internship (6th year)

MBBS Specializations

Some popular specializations in MBBS include:

  • General Medicine
  • General Surgery
  • Pediatrics
  • Obstetrics and Gynecology
  • Orthopedics
  • Psychiatry

Top Medical Colleges in India

Some top medical colleges in India include:

  • All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS), New Delhi
  • Christian Medical College (CMC), Vellore
  • Armed Forces Medical College (AFMC), Pune
  • Grant Medical College, Mumbai
  • Madras Medical College, Chennai

Career Opportunities

MBBS graduates can pursue various career paths, including:

  • General Physician
  • Surgeon
  • Specialist Doctor
  • Medical Researcher
  • Public Health Specialist

Salary Range

The salary range for MBBS graduates varies depending on the specialization, location, and experience.

Here are some approximate salary ranges:

  • Entry-level: ₹6-10 lakhs per annum
  • Mid-level: ₹15-25 lakhs per annum
  • Senior-level: ₹30-50 lakhs per annum
